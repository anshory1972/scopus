🚨

Rapid Concentration in At-Risk Journals

Between 2015 and 2020, Indonesian authors published 6× more papers in journals that Scopus would later discontinue, growing from 1,740 to 12,127 annually. At peak, these journals absorbed nearly 1-in-4 Indonesian Scopus papers.

📊

The 2021 Discontinuation Cliff

Output in discontinued journals dropped 62% in a single year (2020→2021), from 12,127 to 4,651 papers. This cliff directly reflects Scopus's mass discontinuation wave, instantly removing years of Indonesian research from the indexed literature.

🌏

Regional Context

Indonesia's exposure to discontinued journals is significantly higher than Malaysia and Thailand relative to total output, and comparable to Vietnam in share terms, suggesting a systemic preference for quantity over journal quality in the growth phase.

✅

Post-2021 Recovery Signal

Since 2021, the volume has stabilised at 3,000–4,000 papers per year — lower than peak but showing a structural floor. This may indicate continued use of lower-tier journals alongside broader institutional awareness of Scopus quality thresholds.

Data & Methodology
  • Journal list: Scopus Source List May 2026 — "Discontinued Titles" sheet (1,142 journals)
  • Query: AFFILCOUNTRY({country}) AND SOURCE-ID({id1} OR {id2} OR …) AND PUBYEAR = {year}
  • API: Scopus Search API (Elsevier) · Fetched June 2026 · Batched in groups of 100 source IDs
  • Benchmark countries: Malaysia, Thailand, India, Vietnam — same source ID list applied
  • Share of output denominators: approximate annual Scopus country totals
