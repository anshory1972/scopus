Interpretation — Indonesian academic output in soon-to-be-discontinued journals grew rapidly from 1,740 papers in 2015 to a peak of 12,127 in 2020, before collapsing 62% in 2021 as Scopus enacted its largest discontinuation wave. The trend reflects the impact of predatory and low-quality journal publishing on Indonesian research visibility: papers in discontinued journals are effectively removed from the Scopus citation ecosystem.
