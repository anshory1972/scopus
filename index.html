📌 Interpretation: Papers by Indonesian authors in soon-to-be-discontinued journals surged from 1,740 in 2015 to a peak of ~12,127 in 2020, then fell sharply as Scopus began removing low-quality titles. The 2021 cliff reflects the discontinuation wave. This trend highlights the risk of publishing in journals that later lose Scopus indexing, which directly reduces the visibility and citation impact of Indonesian research.
